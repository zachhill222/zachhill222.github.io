About Me

Zachary Hilliard recieved his Ph.D. in Mathematics from Washington State University in 2020 under the direction of Professor Lynn Schreyer and was a postdoc at Oregon State University from September of 2022 to Decmenber of 2023. Previously he recieved his B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Washington State University in 2014. He has also interned at Pacific Northwest National Laboratories for several years and taught at Grove Christian School in Richmond, VA.

Zachary's research interests include partial differential equations (PDEs), finite element methods (FEM), and nonlinear optimization. Topics such as the Cahn-Hilliard equation and other gradient descent and phase-field models are of particular interest.

Currently, Zachary is an Assistant Professor at Regent University.
