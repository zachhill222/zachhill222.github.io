About Me

Zachary Hilliard recieved his Ph.D. in Mathematics from Washington State University in 2020 under the direction of Professor Lynn Schreyer. He was a postdoc at Oregon State University from September of 2022 to Decmenber of 2023 and from 2020 to 2021 at Washington State University as a courtesy. Previously he recieved his B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Washington State University in 2014. He has also interned at Pacific Northwest National Laboratories for six years of the years between 2011 and 2018. Additionally, he taught mathematics and coached volleyball at Grove Christian School in Richmond, VA from Fall 2020 to Spring 2022.

Zachary's research interests include partial differential equations (PDEs), finite element methods (FEM), and nonlinear optimization. Topics such as the Cahn-Hilliard equation and other gradient descent or phase-field models are of particular interest.

Since January 2024, Zachary is an Assistant Professor of Applied Mathematics at Regent University.
